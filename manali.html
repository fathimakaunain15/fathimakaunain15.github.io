Manali — Ultimate Travel Guide

Himalayan snow peaks, pine forests, riverside cafés, adventure sports and cosy homestays — plan the perfect Manali holiday with this complete premium guide.

Best time: Oct–Feb (snow) · Mar–Jun Ideal trip: 4–6 days Altitude: ~2,050 m

Quick Overview

Manali, in Himachal Pradesh, sits on the banks of the Beas River and is surrounded by cedar forests and snow-capped peaks. It’s a favourite for honeymoons, family trips, backpackers and first-time Himalayan travellers.

Top Attractions

  • Old Manali & riverside cafés
  • Solang Valley (snow & adventure)
  • Atal Tunnel & Sissu landscapes
  • Hadimba Temple & cedar forest
  • Naggar Castle & Jana waterfall
  • Vashisht village & hot springs

Travel Tips

  • Carry layers — evenings are cold even in April–May.
  • Keep 1 buffer day in winter in case of heavy snowfall.
  • Use licensed operators for paragliding and activities.
  • Avoid standing too close to the river during high flow.

3-Day Manali Itinerary

Day 1 — Arrival & Old Manali

Check in, relax by the Beas River, explore Old Manali cafés, live music spots and sunset from the bridge.

Day 2 — Solang Valley & Adventure

Head to Solang Valley for snow play (winter), cable car, zorbing or paragliding (seasonal). Evening at Mall Road.

Day 3 — Naggar & Departure

Visit Naggar Castle, Jana waterfall and surrounding villages. Return to Manali and depart in the evening / night bus.

Bonus: For a 5-day trip, add a full day for Atal Tunnel & Sissu plus one slow café day in Old Manali.

How to Reach Manali

  • Nearest Airport: Bhuntar (Kullu) – about 50 km from Manali.
  • By Road: Overnight Volvo from Delhi / Chandigarh is the most popular option.
  • From Delhi: Approx. 12–14 hours by bus / car (via Chandigarh & Mandi).
  • From Chandigarh: Approx. 8–9 hours by road.
