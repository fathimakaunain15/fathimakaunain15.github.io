Meghalaya — Mist, Waterfalls & Living Roots

Shillong, Cherrapunji (Sohra), Dawki & Mawlynnong — waterfalls, caves, living root bridges and dramatic monsoon landscapes. Premium guide with gallery & itinerary.

Northeast India Famous: Falls, Roots & Monsoon Best time: Sep–May (avoid heavy monsoon if you hate rains)

Quick Overview

Meghalaya — literally "abode of clouds" — is a green, water-laced state with some of India's highest rainfall, stunning waterfalls (Nohkalikai, Seven Sisters), living root bridges in Cherrapunji & Mawlynnong (village ranked Asia's cleanest), and clear river waters at Dawki.

Top Attractions

  • Cherrapunji (Sohra) – Nohkalikai Falls, root bridges
  • Shillong – Cathedral, Elephant Falls, cafes & viewpoint
  • Dawki – Clear Umngot River & boating
  • Mawlynnong – Living Root Bridge & clean village
  • Mawsmai Caves – limestone caves & trails

Quick Tips

  • Monsoon (Jun–Aug) is dramatic but very wet — roads can be slippery.
  • Local Khasi & Garo culture is warm — ask before photographing people.
  • Carry rainproof gear and waterproof phone cases during rains.

3-Day Itinerary (Shillong → Cherrapunji → Dawki)

Day 1 — Shillong: Arrive & Explore

Reach Shillong (via Guwahati). Visit Shillong Peak, Elephant Falls, Police Bazaar for cafes & local life. Overnight in Shillong.

Day 2 — Cherrapunji (Sohra): Waterfalls & Root Bridges

Drive to Cherrapunji. Visit Nohkalikai Falls, Mawsmai Caves, and the famous living root bridges (short treks/steps). Overnight near Cherrapunji.

Day 3 — Dawki & Mawlynnong: River & Clean Village

Drive to Dawki for a clear water boat ride on Umngot River. Visit Mawlynnong, the cleanest village with skywalk & root bridges. Return to Shillong or Guwahati.

Things To Do & Experiences

Nature & Adventure

  • Boat ride on Umngot River (Dawki) — crystal clear water
  • Walk the living root bridges (wear good shoes)
  • Explore limestone caves (Mawsmai)
  • Watch breathtaking waterfalls & viewpoints

Culture & Food

  • Try local Khasi cuisine & bamboo shoot dishes
  • Visit local craft shops & tea stalls in Shillong
  • Interact respectfully with village communities

How to Reach

  • By Air: Fly to Guwahati (IXB) then drive ~3–4 hrs to Shillong
  • By Road: Shillong is connected to Guwahati & nearby towns by good roads
  • By Train: Nearest major railhead: Guwahati

Packing & Safety

  • Waterproof jacket & quick-dry clothing; sturdy walking shoes for root bridges.
  • Carry insect repellent and basic medicine; mobile signals may be patchy in remote spots.
  • Avoid risky treks during heavy rainfall; use local guides where needed.

Local Tips

  • Visit Mawlynnong early in the day to avoid crowds.
  • Respect local rules about littering — villages take pride in cleanliness.
  • Hire local boatmen for Dawki — they know the safe spots and best photo angles.
