Quick Overview

Zermatt is a car-free mountain village at the foot of the Matterhorn — a luxury alpine destination famous for skiing, scenic trains and dramatic panoramas.

Top Highlights

  • Matterhorn viewpoint & photography
  • Gornergrat Railway — panoramic cogwheel ride
  • Glacier Paradise (Klein Matterhorn)
  • World-class skiing & heli-skiing
  • Chalet stays, Swiss cuisine & fondue

Tips

  • Zermatt is car-free — park in Täsch and take shuttle train.
  • Book Gornergrat tickets in advance for sunrise trips.
  • Layer up — alpine weather is changeable.
  • Many mountain lifts close in off-season — check local timetables.

3-Day Matterhorn Itinerary

Day 1 — Arrival & Village Walk

Arrive in Zermatt, stroll the car-free village, visit the Matterhorn Museum, enjoy a cozy chalet dinner with fondue.

Day 2 — Gornergrat Railway & Sunrise

Take the Gornergrat cogwheel train early for sunrise views of the Matterhorn. Afternoon: hike or take panorama lifts. Evening spa.

Day 3 — Glacier Paradise & Skiing

Visit Klein Matterhorn (Glacier Paradise) — panoramic glacier platform. Optional skiing or helicopter scenic flight.

Activities & Experiences

Winter & Snow

  • Downhill & cross-country skiing
  • Snowshoeing & guided glacier hikes
  • Heli-skiing (advanced only)

Summer & Hikes

  • Alpine hikes with Matterhorn views
  • Mountain biking trails
  • Scenic rail and cable car rides

Quick Facts

Altitude: Zermatt village ~1,620 m • Matterhorn summit 4,478 m

Access: Park in Täsch (car lot) & take shuttle train to Zermatt (≈12 min)

Top Chalets & Hotels

Luxury Alpine Chalet

Private sauna, Matterhorn terrace views.

Mountain Spa Hotel

Wellness center & panoramic windows.

How to Reach

  • Fly to Zürich/Geneva • Train to Visp • Change for Matterhorn Gotthard Bahn to Zermatt
  • Or drive to Täsch and shuttle to Zermatt

Packing & Safety

  • Warm layers, sunglasses & sunscreen (snow glare).
  • Proper boots for hiking; crampons for icy conditions.
  • High-altitude caution — hydrate & acclimatize.
