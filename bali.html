Bali Travel Guide (2026) – 6 Day Itinerary, Budget & Insider Tips

Bali, Indonesia’s most famous island, is a paradise of beaches, rice terraces, temples and vibrant culture. It offers a perfect mix of relaxation, adventure and spirituality.

Whether you're planning a honeymoon, solo trip or friends’ getaway, this guide will help you with a complete 6-day Bali itinerary, budget estimate and essential travel tips.

Why Visit Bali?

Bali is known for its stunning landscapes, affordable luxury villas, surfing beaches, and rich Hindu culture. It’s one of the best international destinations for Indian travelers due to easy visa access.

Best Time to Visit Bali

  • April – October: Dry season (best time)
  • November – March: Rainy but less crowded

6-Day Bali Itinerary

Day 1 – Seminyak

Relax at Seminyak Beach, enjoy beach clubs, and explore sunset views.

Day 2 – Uluwatu

Visit Uluwatu Temple and watch Kecak dance performance. Explore cliffside beaches.

Day 3 – Ubud

Explore rice terraces, Tegenungan waterfall, and Sacred Monkey Forest.

Day 4 – Mount Batur

Early morning volcano sunrise trek. Relax at hot springs.

Day 5 – Nusa Penida

Visit Kelingking Beach and Angel’s Billabong. One of Bali’s most photogenic spots.

Day 6 – Shopping & Relaxation

Explore local markets and enjoy spa treatments.

Bali Budget (Per Person – 6 Days)

  • Flights: ₹15,000 – ₹25,000
  • Stay (Villa): ₹10,000 – ₹20,000
  • Transport: ₹6,000
  • Food: ₹6,000 – ₹8,000
  • Activities: ₹5,000 – ₹8,000

Total Estimated Budget: ₹45,000 – ₹70,000

Where to Stay in Bali

  • Budget: Kuta guesthouses
  • Mid-range: Seminyak hotels
  • Luxury: Private pool villas in Ubud

What to Eat in Bali

  • Nasi Goreng
  • Mie Goreng
  • Satay skewers
  • Fresh tropical smoothies

Common Travel Mistakes

  • Underestimating traffic time
  • Not carrying international driving permit
  • Ignoring temple dress codes
  • Booking tours without checking reviews

FAQs

Is Bali expensive?
Bali can be affordable compared to other international destinations.

Do Indians need visa for Bali?
Visa-on-arrival is available for Indian travelers.

How many days are enough?
6–7 days are ideal.

Final Thoughts

Bali is one of the most versatile travel destinations in Asia. With proper planning, it offers incredible value and unforgettable memories.
