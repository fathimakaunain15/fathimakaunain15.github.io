Andaman Travel Guide (2026) – 5 Day Itinerary, Budget & Island Tips

The Andaman Islands are one of India’s most breathtaking tropical destinations. With crystal-clear turquoise waters, white sand beaches, coral reefs, and peaceful island life, Andaman feels like an international beach destination within India.

If you're planning a trip to Andaman, this guide will help you with a complete 5-day itinerary, estimated budget, where to stay, what to eat, and important travel mistakes to avoid.

Why Visit Andaman?

Andaman is ideal for honeymooners, couples, families, and even solo travelers. It offers clean beaches, adventure water sports, and one of the best scuba diving experiences in India.

Best Time to Visit Andaman

  • October – April: Best season (pleasant weather)
  • May – September: Monsoon, fewer tourists but rough sea

5-Day Andaman Itinerary

Day 1 – Port Blair

Arrive at Port Blair. Visit Cellular Jail and attend the Light & Sound show. Relax at Corbyn’s Cove Beach in the evening.

Day 2 – Havelock Island

Take ferry to Havelock. Visit Radhanagar Beach (one of Asia’s best beaches). Enjoy sunset photography.

Day 3 – Water Activities

Go for scuba diving or snorkeling at Elephant Beach. This is the highlight of Andaman.

Day 4 – Neil Island

Visit Natural Bridge and Bharatpur Beach. Neil Island is quieter and more peaceful.

Day 5 – Return & Local Shopping

Return to Port Blair. Buy local souvenirs like shell crafts and spices.

Andaman Budget (Per Person – 5 Days)

  • Flights: ₹8,000 – ₹15,000
  • Stay: ₹6,000 – ₹12,000
  • Ferry & Local Transport: ₹3,000
  • Food: ₹4,000 – ₹6,000
  • Scuba Diving: ₹3,500 – ₹5,000

Total Estimated Budget: ₹20,000 – ₹35,000

Where to Stay in Andaman

  • Budget: Guesthouses in Port Blair
  • Mid-range: Havelock beach resorts
  • Luxury: Private beach villas

What to Eat in Andaman

  • Fresh seafood curry
  • Grilled fish & prawns
  • Coconut-based dishes
  • Tropical fruits

Travel Mistakes to Avoid

  • Booking ferries late during peak season
  • Not checking weather before scuba diving
  • Overpacking (travel light)
  • Ignoring travel insurance

FAQs About Andaman

Is Andaman expensive?
It can be mid-range, but proper planning keeps costs reasonable.

Is passport required?
No, Indian citizens don’t need a passport for Andaman.

Is scuba diving safe?
Yes, when done with certified instructors.

Final Thoughts

Andaman is perfect for those seeking peaceful beaches and underwater adventures. If you want a tropical escape without leaving India, Andaman is a fantastic choice.
