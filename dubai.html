Dubai Travel Guide (2026) – 5 Day Itinerary, Budget & Insider Tips

Dubai is one of the most modern and luxurious cities in the world. Known for its skyscrapers, shopping malls, desert safaris and futuristic attractions, Dubai offers a unique blend of tradition and innovation.

If you are planning your first Dubai trip, this guide covers a practical 5-day itinerary, realistic budget estimate, where to stay and smart tips to avoid overspending.

Why Visit Dubai?

Dubai is perfect for travelers who enjoy modern cities, luxury experiences and organized infrastructure. It is safe, well-connected and ideal for short international trips from India.

Best Time to Visit Dubai

  • November – March: Best weather (cool & pleasant)
  • April – October: Very hot, but hotel prices are lower

5-Day Dubai Itinerary

Day 1 – Downtown Dubai

Visit Burj Khalifa observation deck, Dubai Mall and watch the Dubai Fountain show.

Day 2 – Desert Safari

Book a desert safari with dune bashing, camel ride and traditional dinner. This is one of Dubai’s top experiences.

Day 3 – Marina & Beaches

Explore Dubai Marina, JBR Beach and take a yacht cruise.

Day 4 – Cultural Side

Visit Al Fahidi historical district, Dubai Creek and traditional souks.

Day 5 – Palm Jumeirah & Shopping

Explore Palm Jumeirah and relax at Atlantis area. Last-minute shopping before departure.

Dubai Budget (Per Person – 5 Days)

  • Flights: ₹15,000 – ₹25,000
  • Hotel: ₹15,000 – ₹30,000
  • Food: ₹6,000 – ₹10,000
  • Transport: ₹4,000
  • Activities: ₹10,000 – ₹15,000

Total Estimated Budget: ₹50,000 – ₹85,000

Where to Stay in Dubai

  • Budget: Deira & Bur Dubai
  • Mid-range: Business Bay
  • Luxury: Downtown & Palm Jumeirah

What to Eat in Dubai

  • Shawarma
  • Falafel
  • Arabic mezze platters
  • International cuisines

Common Travel Mistakes

  • Not booking Burj Khalifa tickets in advance
  • Visiting during extreme summer without planning
  • Overspending on taxis instead of using metro
  • Ignoring local dress rules in public areas

FAQs

Is Dubai expensive?
Dubai can be mid-range to expensive depending on your choices.

Is visa required for Indians?
Yes, a tourist visa is required before travel.

How many days are enough?
4–5 days are perfect for major attractions.

Final Thoughts

Dubai is a well-planned city offering world-class experiences. With smart planning, it can be an exciting and smooth international trip.
