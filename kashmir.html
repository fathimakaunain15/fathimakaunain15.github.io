Quick Overview

Kashmir is famed for its serene lakes, snow-capped peaks and Mughal gardens. Srinagar (Dal Lake) is the heart; Gulmarg is the skiing hub; Pahalgam & Sonmarg are scenic valleys for nature lovers.

Top Attractions

  • Dal Lake & Shikara rides (Srinagar)
  • Gulmarg Gondola – cable car to Apharwat
  • Pahalgam & Betaab Valley – river valleys & treks
  • Sonmarg – gateway to glaciers
  • Mughal Gardens – Nishat & Shalimar Bagh

Quick Tips

  • Carry layered warm clothes in winter and light jackets in summer.
  • Book houseboats early in peak season.
  • Permits may be required for certain high passes (check local rules).
  • Respect local customs, avoid restricted areas, and follow security advice.

4-Day Best Itinerary

Day 1 — Srinagar: Arrival & Houseboat

Arrive in Srinagar, check into a houseboat near Dal Lake. Evening shikara ride and sunset views. Walk the Mughal Gardens if time permits.

Day 2 — Gulmarg: Gondola & Snow

Drive to Gulmarg (1.5–2 hrs). Take the Gulmarg Gondola up to Apharwat for snow sports or panoramic views. Return to Srinagar or stay in Gulmarg.

Day 3 — Pahalgam: Valleys & Short Treks

Drive to Pahalgam (approx 2–3 hrs). Explore Betaab Valley, Aru Valley; short scenic walks and riverside cafes.

Day 4 — Sonmarg or Local Markets

Option A: Day trip to Sonmarg for glacier views. Option B: Shop local Kashmiri handicrafts & saffron products in Srinagar and prepare to depart.

  • By Air: Srinagar International Airport (SXR)
  • By Road: Well-connected roads from Srinagar to Gulmarg, Pahalgam & Sonmarg
  • By Train: Jammu Tawi then road to Srinagar (train routes limited)

Packing & Safety

  • Winter: heavy woollens, thermals, hand warmers; Summer: jackets for nights.
  • Carry necessary medicines; check weather before heading to high passes.
  • Follow local advice; avoid restricted areas and always travel with reliable transport.

Local Tips

  • Try authentic Kashmiri saffron and dry fruits (buy from reputable shops).
  • Respect local culture and photography rules near military areas.
  • Always keep scanned copies of your ID and travel permits.
