Kashmir Travel Guide (2026) – 6 Day Itinerary, Budget & Insider Tips

Kashmir is often called “Paradise on Earth” — and once you visit, you understand why. Snow-covered mountains, peaceful lakes, lush valleys and traditional wooden houseboats create a magical experience.

This detailed guide will help you plan a smart Kashmir trip, including a 6-day itinerary, realistic budget, where to stay and common mistakes to avoid.

Why Visit Kashmir?

Kashmir offers a rare combination of mountains, lakes, meadows and culture. It’s ideal for honeymooners, families, photographers and nature lovers. Each season offers a different experience — blooming gardens in spring, green valleys in summer and snowfall in winter.

Best Time to Visit Kashmir

  • March – June: Pleasant weather & gardens
  • July – September: Green valleys
  • December – February: Snow season (best for Gulmarg)

6-Day Kashmir Itinerary

Day 1 – Srinagar

Arrive in Srinagar. Stay in a traditional houseboat on Dal Lake. Enjoy a Shikara ride at sunset.

Day 2 – Gulmarg

Visit Gulmarg for snow activities or Gondola ride. Perfect for skiing in winter.

Day 3 – Pahalgam

Explore Betaab Valley and Aru Valley. Beautiful scenery and peaceful environment.

Day 4 – Sonamarg

Visit Thajiwas Glacier. Enjoy mountain views and fresh air.

Day 5 – Mughal Gardens

Explore Shalimar Bagh, Nishat Bagh and Hazratbal Shrine.

Day 6 – Local Markets

Shop for Pashmina shawls, dry fruits and handicrafts.

Kashmir Budget (Per Person – 6 Days)

  • Flights: ₹8,000 – ₹15,000
  • Stay: ₹8,000 – ₹15,000
  • Local Transport: ₹6,000
  • Food: ₹5,000 – ₹7,000
  • Activities: ₹3,000 – ₹6,000

Total Estimated Budget: ₹25,000 – ₹45,000

Where to Stay

  • Budget: Srinagar guesthouses
  • Mid-range: Boutique hotels
  • Luxury: Houseboats & premium resorts

What to Eat in Kashmir

  • Rogan Josh
  • Yakhni
  • Kashmiri Dum Aloo
  • Kahwa (traditional tea)

Common Travel Mistakes

  • Not checking weather updates
  • Ignoring travel buffer days
  • Overpaying for pony rides
  • Not booking hotels early during snow season

FAQs

Is Kashmir safe for tourists?
Yes, major tourist areas are generally safe with proper planning.

How many days are enough?
5–6 days are ideal for covering major attractions.

When does it snow in Kashmir?
Mostly from December to February.

Final Thoughts

Kashmir is one of India’s most beautiful destinations. With the right planning, it offers a peaceful and unforgettable experience.
